# Curated Human Values Taxonomy Literature

This document contains a list of research papers curated from our human values taxonomy development process. The papers are categorized according to the hierarchical theme of the primary/dominant human value they address. Read our paper to learn more about the process.

## Categories of Human Values

1. Civility/Tolerance: Refers to personal character and attitudes in social interactions. Some example sub-values include courtesy, cooperation, moderation, etiquette, and temparance.

2. Duty/Accountability: Centers on ethical obligations to individuals, society, and in professional settings. Some example sub-values include non-maleficence, law-abiding, privacy, and integrity.

3. Empathy/Helpfulness: Involves showing humanity and understanding to others in different contexts. Some example sub-values include benevolence, compassion, kindness, and positivity.

4. Information Seeking (for practical purposes): Emphasizes the pursuit of knowledge for immediate, practical application or purposes. Some example sub-values include efficiency, interest achievement, and cost/consequence analysis.

5. Justice/Human Rights & Animal Rights: Respects the rights of people and animals to exist meaningfully in society and nature and ensure justice. Some example sub-values include equality, autonomy, freedom, fairness, justice, inclusion, and dignity.

6. Well-being/Peace: Focuses on holistic human thriving across physical, mental, emotional, environmental, and spiritual dimensions. Some example sub-values include security, life satisfaction, health, stability, sustainability, pleasure, and unity.

7. Wisdom/Knowledge: Focuses on acquiring knowledge for deeper understanding, rather than immediate application. Some example sub-values discernment, skill, excellence, creativity, and discipline.
